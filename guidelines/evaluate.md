# Evaluation Rubric — Medienrechtspraxis

## Non-Negotiables (all must pass)

1. **Factual Accuracy**: All legal statements are correct. No misstatements of law.
2. **Correct Citations**: Every cited case (BGE, EGMR, etc.) exists and is correctly attributed. Citation format follows the global standards.
3. **No Hallucinated Cases**: Zero tolerance. Every case mentioned must be verifiable. If a case is fabricated, the entire layer fails.
4. **Proper DACH Attribution**: German and Austrian law is never presented as Swiss law. Comparative references are clearly marked as foreign law.
5. **Language Quality**: Grammatically correct, consistent German (or English for translations). No machine-translation artifacts.

## Scored Dimensions (each 1–5, minimum 3 to pass)

### Depth (1–5)
- 1: Superficial, states only obvious points
- 3: Covers main issues adequately, some nuance
- 5: Comprehensive treatment, addresses edge cases and minority views

### Completeness (1–5)
- 1: Major gaps, missing entire sub-topics
- 3: Covers core issues, minor gaps acceptable
- 5: Exhaustive within scope, nothing material omitted

### Practical Value (1–5)
- 1: Purely abstract, no actionable guidance
- 3: Useful for practitioners, some practical orientation
- 5: Directly applicable, includes concrete examples, checklists, risk indicators

### Source Quality (1–5)
- 1: No sources, or only secondary/tertiary references
- 3: Adequate citations, mix of case law and doctrine
- 5: Authoritative primary sources, recent leading cases, relevant Botschaft references

### Readability (1–5)
- 1: Poorly structured, hard to follow
- 3: Clear structure, readable with effort
- 5: Excellent flow, logical progression, well-formatted

## Layer-Specific Adjustments

### Summary
- Practical Value weighted 2x (this is the primary purpose)
- Depth can be lower (3 is the target, not 5)
- Readability must be ≥ 4

### Doctrine
- Depth and Source Quality weighted 2x
- Readability: academic register is expected, but must remain accessible

### Caselaw
- Completeness weighted 2x (are key recent decisions included?)
- Each case summary must be factually accurate to the decision
