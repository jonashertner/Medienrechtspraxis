# Global Quality Standards — Medienrechtspraxis

## Academic Excellence

All content must meet the standard of a leading Swiss legal commentary (Kommentar). Content is authoritative, current, and practically useful.

## Citation Format

- **BGer**: BGE 127 III 481 (published), Urteil 5A_123/2024 (unpublished)
- **EGMR**: EGMR, Urteil vom 7.2.2012, Nr. 40660/08 — Von Hannover gegen Deutschland (Nr. 2)
- **UBI**: UBI, Entscheid b. 859 vom 19.10.2023
- **German courts**: BGH, Urteil vom 15.9.2015, VI ZR 175/14; BVerfG, 1 BvR 16/13
- **Austrian courts**: OGH 6 Ob 123/20f; VfGH G 12/2021
- **Doctrine**: Author, Title, Edition Year, page/Rz. (e.g., Meili, BSK ZGB I, 7. Aufl. 2022, Art. 28 N 15)

## DACH Comparison Requirements

Where relevant, include brief comparative references to German and Austrian law. Purpose: show Swiss practitioners how neighbouring jurisdictions handle the same issue. Always attribute clearly: "In Deutschland gilt gemäss § 823 BGB..." / "Das österreichische Recht sieht in § 16 ABGB..."

## Layer-Specific Rules

### Summary Layer
- Language: clear, direct German. No legalese where avoidable.
- Length: 300–500 words.
- No footnotes, no Randziffern.
- Practical focus: answer "What can I do?" and "What are the risks?"
- Include checklist format where it helps (✓ / ✗ structure).

### Doctrine Layer
- Language: academic German, appropriate for Kommentar-level commentary.
- Randziffern: N. 1, N. 2, etc. — continuous numbering within the topic.
- Length: 1500–3000 words.
- Minimum 5 cited sources (BGE, doctrine, Botschaft).
- Structure: Rechtsgrundlage → Voraussetzungen → Rechtsfolgen → DACH-Vergleich → Praxishinweise.
- Discuss minority opinions and open questions where relevant.

### Caselaw Layer
- Group decisions thematically.
- Within each group: chronological, most recent first.
- Per decision: full citation, date, one-paragraph summary (3–5 sentences), significance rating.
- Significance: ★ (routine application), ★★ (clarification/development), ★★★ (Leitentscheid).
- Courts: BGer, EGMR, UBI, cantonal supreme courts.

## Language Quality

- No anglicisms where German terms exist.
- Consistent terminology within and across topics.
- Active voice preferred.
- Precise legal terminology — no vague approximations.

## Factual Integrity

- Every case citation must correspond to a real, verifiable decision.
- Every statute reference must be current (check for recent revisions).
- No hallucinated sources — if uncertain, omit rather than fabricate.
- Clearly distinguish settled law from disputed positions.
